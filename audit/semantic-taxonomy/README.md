# Semantic Taxonomy Evidence

Generated by `.venv\\Scripts\\python.exe scripts\\build_semantic_taxonomy.py` from local repository
evidence. Regenerate the artifacts whenever the master dataset, CID parsing, or taxonomy rules change.

## Artifacts

- `CENSUS.md` — coverage, group, tag, attribute, and review counts.
- `visibility-matrix.csv` — one row per establishment showing actual discovery membership.
- `review-queue.json` — unresolved cross-category cases; currently empty.
- `resolved-primary-categories.csv` — the complete 23-record decision ledger with old category,
  resolved category, disposition, and evidence rationale.
- `evidence-packets.jsonl` — one joined source-text packet per establishment, including CID full-text
  hashes and parsed evidence. This is local evidence, not part of the public release.
- `evidence-poor-records.csv` — records without linked CID full text; prioritized input for a future
  source sweep.
- `unlinked-cid-captures.csv` — unique CID captures not associated with a current master CID.

## Interpretation

The accepted taxonomy is additive. It does not overwrite the canonical primary category. Broad
groups control visitor discovery, specific tags express what the establishment is, and attributes
express supported qualities without redefining its identity. Every accepted assertion retains its
source, deterministic rule, confidence, and matched evidence.

All 23 initially flagged cross-category identities have been resolved: 19 canonical corrections and
four documented retentions. The unresolved review queue is empty. Legitimate mixed-use businesses
continue to receive additive secondary discovery without losing their reviewed primary identity.
